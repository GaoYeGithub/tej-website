Hatsune Miku Info: officially code-named CV01; a vocaloid software performing as a idol


Who?

Hatsune Miku is a virtual singer and character developed by Crypton Future Media. She's portrayed as a 16-year-old girl with long turquoise twin-tails, often seen in futuristic or stylish attire.
Despite being fictional, she has become a global icon, with fans treating her like a real pop star.


What?

Hatsune Miku is the personification of a Vocaloid—a voice synthesizer software that allows users to input melodies and lyrics to create songs.
Her voice was created using samples from Japanese voice actress Saki Fujita.
She has been used in over 100,000 songs and fan-made videos, spanning genres and styles.

How?

  1. Miku was launched in 2007 as part of Yamaha's Vocaloid 2 engine, distributed by Crypton Future Media.
  2. Crypton packaged the software with a character design and backstory to make it more appealing and relatable.
  3. The public could freely create music and videos using her voice and image, which led to an explosion of user-generated content.
  4. Platforms like Niconico and YouTube helped her go viral.
  5. She performs live holographic concerts around the world with a backing band, thanks to projection and motion capture technology.

Inspiration

What Inspire Miku?!

  • The idea was inspired by the desire to create a new kind of music tool—one where fans could become creators.
  • Crypton also aimed to blend technology, music, and culture into something accessible and expressive.
  • Miku’s design and concept were influenced by anime and futuristic cyber aesthetics, along with the culture of open collaboration in the creative community.
